I hold a B.Sc in Mathematics with a concentration in Statistics. My background is primarily focused in statistical modeling and high-performance data engineering, supported by a minor in Economics. Thus it is no surprise that I am interested in the intersection of these fields, and the use of statistical modeling and training to finance. I write about these endevours (among others) in my blog, *The Kernel*.

## Current Focus
I am currently developing an end-to-end quantitative framework utilizing a proprietary non-parametric test to isolate jump and diffusion components to generate high-fidelity labels for our neural forecasting model. We use these predictions to simulate asset prices and predict future regime changes via Monte Carlo methods.

## Workflow & Stack
Most of my numerical computing and statistical analysis is done in Julia. We utilize the speed of the language (and its support for native GPU programming) to analyze millions of rows of data and run intensive calculations within seconds. I also utilize Go to orchestrate our many microservices, ensuring stability and efficiency via its lightweight coroutines. Python is used to take advantage of its well-developed and supported ecosystem for model libraries, as well as interfacing with Hugging Face.

I do most of my development on Linux, specifically an Arch (btw)/Hyprland setup. As much as I value its highly customizable and keyboard-driven workflow for both professional and personal computing, reproducibility is a must across all projects and machines, especially for deployment. Docker is the obvious choice to ensure that my research and microservices remain consistent and packaged with the correct dependencies and versions across all environments.

## Interests
I consider myself an information-junkie. I love learning about a wide variety of things, whether it be the context behind the existential dread felt in a meaningless system found in Kafka's literature, or why emphasizing the stretch at the bottom of the range aids in hypertrophy.

Beyond technical documentation, I spend a significant amount of time on creative writing. I mostly focus on short stories, with ideas of magical realism, character-driven plots, and blurring the lines between prose and poetry capturing my interests lately. Given my interest in creating these works, it should be no surprise that I also enjoy consuming them as well. My current reading list is an odd mix across a multitude of genres, from Philosophy and textbooks to stories about a man who doesn't know how to read or write trying to find a lost cat.

I also enjoy weightlifting as a physical pursuit, with the gym being a consistent anchor for me; a space to release stress and serve as a meditative contrast to the abstract nature of my work. I am also lucky enough to live an hour away from the Rockies and Banff National Park, giving me a winter wonderland to explore and countless hiking opportunities.
